Violin

Nikki
Naghavi

University: New England Conservatory

Music: 18-year-old Nikki Naghavi was born in Dallas, Texas. She started violin lessons at the age of 4 with Nicolette Solomon, continued her studies with Emanuel Borok, and currently studies at the New England Conservatory of Music under the tutelage of Ayano Ninomiya. She had her orchestral debut in January 2018 with the Baylor Chamber Orchestra, and she soloed with the Plano Symphony in March of that year. Nikki also won 3rd prize at the Lynn Harrel Competition in 2018. Nikki was selected as concertmaster of the Texas All-State Symphony orchestra in 2017 and was also the concertmaster of the Greater Dallas Youth Orchestra. In the summer of 2018, Nikki attended and performed several times at the Bowdoin International Music Festival and in the summer of 2019, she was given the opportunity to tour Europe as principal second violinist of the National Youth Orchestra of the USA. She has been fortunate to have had masterclasses with many renown pedagogues such as Paul Kantor, Martin Beaver, Robert McDuffie, Michael Vaiman, Meg Freivogel, and Annie Fullard, among others. Nikki has performed on the radio show, From The Top in June 2019 and was also selected as a collegiate ambassador for the Boston Symphony Orchestra’s 2019-2020 season. Aside from playing the violin, Nikki enjoys traveling and exploring new cultures, as well as spending time with her friends and family. As a founding member of TTS, Nikki is excited to see the potential impact it will have on the lives of music students across America.

Fun Facts: I love to travel and eat new food I am an only child I am CPR & BLS certified

Valentina
Paolucci

University: University of Miami, Frost School of Music

Music: Valentina Paolucci is an 19 year old violinist and currently attends Frost School of Music in University of Miami under the tutelage of Charles Castleman. Valentina’s orchestral experiences include participating in the National Youth Orchestra of the United States of America in 2019, New World Symphony Side-by-Side program under the direction of Michael Tilson Thomas from the years 2017-2019 and being accepted into the Florida All-State Orchestra every year eligible from 2014-2019. Valentina won first place prizes in the 2018 State-Level Florida Federation Concerto Competition, FMTA District 6 2019 Concerto, and the Palm Beach Symphony’s “B-Major” Competition, where she was awarded an antique Italian violin. Additionally, Valentina was awarded a 1,000 prize from the National League of American Pen Women for her music composition. Along with winning second place prizes in the FMTA District 6 2018 Concerto Competition and the Anointed Easter Music Competition in Miami, Valentina has finalized in the Virtuosi Violin Competition and South Florida Young Artists Concerto Competition.

Fun Facts: I like to spend time outside and read. I like to do math. I love flowers. I love Shostakovich and Beethoven symphonies.

Haeun
Moon

University: Harvard University

Music: Haeun Moon is 18 year-old violinist from Waco, TX. She currently studies with Soovin Kim and attends Harvard University. Prior to that, she studied violin with Brian Lewis, professor of UT Austin. Haeun has won numerous competitions including the Austin Symphony Concerto Competition, the Fort Bend Symphony Concerto Competition, the Houston Civic Symphony Young Artist Concerto Competition, the Waco Symphony Youth Orchestra Concerto Competition, and performed with those orchestras. She was also featured as soloist with the Fort Worth Symphony, East Oregon Symphony, and Ottawa Chamber Orchestra. During the past summer seasons, Haeun has been selected as one of the twelve participants worldwide for the Brian Lewis Young Artist Program (2015, 16, 17) and invited to attend the Great Mountains International Music Festival on a full scholarship in 2016. She has also studied at the International Summer Academy of Mozarteum University at Salzburg, Austria. She also has studied with Miriam Fried, Paul Kantor, Jan Sloman, Boris Brovtsyn, Radu Blidar, Nam Yun Kim, Koichiro Harada, and Joan Kwuon through various master classes. In 2017, Haeun was named as the national first prize winner in the 2017 Music Teachers National Association Senior String Competition final. In addition, she became the grand prize winner at the 2017 Lynn Harrell Concerto Competition and performed with the Dallas Symphony Orchestra at Meyerson Symphony Center. Haeun was also selected as one of ten “Student Artists” worldwide at the 2017 Starling-DeLay Symposium on Violin Studies at the Juilliard School where she had master classes with world-renowned violinists and performed at the Paul Hall. Most recently, Haeun was selected as the concert master of 2018 Texas All-State Symphonic Orchestra, and also a recipient of the From the Top’s Jack Kent Cooke Young Artist Award, a national scholarship for extraordinary young musicians. In addition to receiving a scholarship, she also appeared on NPR’s From the Top twice. It was also in 2018 when Haeun was named one of the National YoungArts Winners for Classical Music. In the summer of 2018, she was invited to Carnegie Hall’s National Youth Orchestra of USA on their tour of Asia, where she served as an associate concertmaster under the baton of Michael Tilson-Thomas. She was invited back in 2019 to tour Europe with the orchestra. In 2019, she performed the Brahms Double Concerto with her twin brother, Hayoung, with both the Balcones Orchestra and the Waco Symphony Orchestra by invitation. Haeun enjoys using her music to reach out to her community. When founded the Mission Waco’s afterschool music program in which she volunteers to teach violin for the underprivileged students. Now, as a founding member of TTS, Haeun is thrilled to teach more students and further the mission of making music more accessible for all. Haeun plays the “Carl Becker and Son 1955” violin, generously loaned by the Honda Violin Trust.

Fun Facts: I love reading, collecting vinyl records, and would love to incorporate the arts into diplomacy (as a career)

Annabella
Paolucci

University: Dreyfoos School of the Arts

Music: Annabella Paolucci is a 17 year-old violinist currently attending A. W. Dreyfoos School of the Arts in West Palm Beach, Florida. Annabella began playing violin at age 5 in the Palm Beach Public Elementary strings program and continued into the Advanced Orchestras at Bak School of the Arts and A. W. Dreyfoos School of the Arts in West Palm Beach playing in renowned concert halls such as Carnegie Hall and the Kennedy Center. Annabella’s other accomplishments include participating as principal second violin in both the New World Symphony’s Side-by-Side program under the direction of Michael Tilson Thomas and the Miami Music Festival conducted by Joel Smirnoff and Mark Gibson, and also attending the Lucerne Music Festival receiving full scholarships for both festivals. Annabella was accepted into the Florida All-State Orchestra every year upon eligibility since seventh grade and was concertmaster in 2017. Annabella currently holds a violin teaching position at Rhythm and Hues Art Center and adores bringing music into the lives of young students. She is also a volunteer at the U.B. Kinsey Elementary School Orchestra and Find The Keys Strings Camp providing tutelage to children who could not otherwise afford it. Apart from classical music, Annabella is a passionate singer, songwriter, and guitarist specializing in latin music.

Fun Facts: I am very passionate about latin music. I love painting and cooking. I have two chihuahuas.
